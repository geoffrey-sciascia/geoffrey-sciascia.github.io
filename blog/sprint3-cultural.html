Emotional Intelligence (EQ)

What is Emotional Intelligence (EQ)?

Emotional Intelligence is the ability to perceive, control, and evaluate emotions, not just in yourself, but also in others. It is closely tied to empathy.

How is it different to IQ?

IQ is used to indentify an individuals who are 'gifted' learners, or inversely, those who require specialised assistance with learning. EQ is used to identify leadership qualities in individuals. EQ looks at;

  • How an individual identifies, evaluates, controls and expresses emotions.
  • In their own emotions; how they perceive, and assess the emotions they are feeling.
  • Others' emotions; how they get others to use emotions to facilitate thinking and understand the emotional meanings.
IQ looks at;
  • The ability to learn, understand and apply information to skills,
  • Logical reasoning,
  • Word comprehension and math skills,
  • Abstract and spatial thinking,
  • And filtering out irrelevant information.
These aspects of intelligence need to work together. Emotional intelligence is an enabler for the development of a higher IQ.

Why is it important?

Emotional intelligence is key to having a successful relationship with yourself and others. A high EQ helps you to build relationships, reduce team stress, defuse conflict and improve job satisfaction. Ultimately, a high EQ means having the potential to increase team productivity and staff retention.
